>Myo is a hunter whose only prey are the ghosts of the past. A loved one, spirited away into the dark maw of [[X - Misc/XXX/The Spiritwood\|The Spiritwood]], and the mysterious creature that haunts it. 
>
>However, such tales could not be further from the truth. There is only one prey that the Hunter has their sights on. The spirit of the wood itself, which feeds on travelers that make their way through the brush, and uses their memories to lure them deeper and deeper into the mist. Their unfortunate moniker of “The Reaper” is only due to their own failures, as the forest claims more victims, they are always a step too late to intervene, marking areas of tragedy with their sigil, both to honor the dead and to warn travelers away. It is said that on certain nights, they can be seen by a certain brook, near a ruined cabin, head bowed low as though mourning something. 
>
>They are always seen wearing a long cloak, hemmed with plants, and their sickle in hand.

>Long before spirits ruled the wood, there was a village, and in that village was a child. Born on a quiet farm beyond the cruel reach of the decay, they spent sunny days tending to the land and long nights exploring the nearby wood with childhood friends. It was a life they found themselves content with. 
>On an ordinary, quiet night, they slipped out of their home, as custom, and donned the ranger's cloak their father wore on his days out. Padding silently under the watchful gaze of the moon, they united with their friends, ready for another adventure into the unknown. As they prepared to venture into the depths, a sudden chill ran over them, and a thought crossed their mind: Turn back. 
>Shaking off the feeling, they hurried after their companions, and lost themselves in venturing under a familiar sky, woven from branches and moonlight. Weaving though the roots, they ventured deeper and deeper, deeper than they'd ever delved before, until the gentle moonlit beams began to disappear, and darkness took their place. There at the center of a world all to themselves, they found a circle of roots emanating from ~~the polyp~~ a small sapling, dimly casting a faint glow around them. They marveled at the sight, and decided as one to take it home as a token of their journey. They dug up the roots, gathered them into their arms, and made haste towards home. 
>They planted it in a quiet corner of the child's farm, taking care to water and watch over it, spending many nights and days huddled around its still form. It took the shape of a tree, and under its branches, they made memories as they counted down the days to adulthood. Sorrow wet the soil, as joy infused it, fertilizing the earth with memoria.

>The tree grew. It's branches rose up to collect the voices in the air. They spent the coming harvest drinking to excess, laughing themselves silly.

>The tree drank too. Twisting roots rose up to take in the moment. Their coming age came and went, childhood dreams of adventure fading to memory.
>
>The tree recorded them all, concentric rings of days past wrapping around a bulbous head.

>Before them a majestic jellyfish loomed above the ground, its root-like tendrils spearing out of the ground. Inside it were their friends. Their memories. Their hopes and dreams.
