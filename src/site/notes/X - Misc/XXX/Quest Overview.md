# Overview
--- 
Vel is given a quest to hunt a mysterious entity in the woods that the people simply call “The Hunter”. Supposedly, it has been seen stalking people who travel through the woods, many of whom disappear without a trace. Vel explores the forest, hunting for this spirit, catching glimpses of it as she draws near, until it is spooked, and attacks her. She battles it fiercely, and whittles down its fearsome aura until all that is left is… a childlike spirit. She is stricken, and as her resolve falters, the world goes dark,  and a fearsome persona takes her over, tearing the screen in two… 
# Notes
--- 
- She’s running from something. It’s crushing her. The pressure of the weight on her shoulders. The responsibilities impaled on her sword. She can’t fail. She has too much to lose. 
- Introduce the quest after Vel explores the forest. Maybe she sees it on a sign post?
	- Your quest is “Do as a Hero Should” 
- She’s afraid of failure. She needs to maintain her self-concept as a hero. She must be perfect. Failure is not an option. Her self-worth is her work. 
- Vel Alter is the manifestation of these expectations. It chases her. Controls her. 

## Quest Design:
- Quests should be open-ended. Tracking quest progress should only encapsulate major beats for the player. 
	- EXAMPLE: Current Quest - The Forest For The Trees 
			- Objective: Find the Reaper.
- Encourage exploration of the world. 
- The UI for the quest tracking should be telegraphed as fallible. 
	- EXAMPLE: When the objective changes to Help the Reaper the UI glitches it back.
- Introduce a ‘twist’ during each quest.
